## The Problem: Your Tank Is a Black Box

Here's the thing about most water tanks — we treat them like a black box. Water goes in, water comes out, and we never really ask what's happening in between.

But underneath that calm surface, a lot is going on. Dust, organic matter, and microbial growth settle at the bottom over weeks and months. Depending on usage, water source, and tank location, this build-up can happen much faster than people expect. Yet most of us still follow the old rule of thumb — "clean it every six months" — without ever checking if that schedule actually matches what's happening inside.

The truth is, every tank is different. A tank serving a large residential building with constant usage accumulates sediment at a completely different rate than a tank in a smaller setup. Sticking to a fixed calendar instead of real conditions is like changing your car's oil based on the date alone, without ever checking the oil itself.

## Why "Six Months" Doesn't Work Anymore

When I started digging deeper into this, one thing became clear: fixed-interval cleaning is a guess, not a solution. It assumes every tank behaves the same way, which simply isn't true.

The result? Some tanks go uncleaned for far too long, silently exposing residents to contaminated water. Others get cleaned more often than necessary, which means unnecessary cost and water wastage. Either way, you're not actually solving the real problem — you're just following a habit.
